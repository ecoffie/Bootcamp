The 5 Most Common Reasons Proposals Lose

Fundamentals

GovCon Giants Session 1: Fundamentals

Evaluators don't read proposals.
They SCORE them.

Every section, every paragraph is evaluated against specific criteria. Write to score points.

How Evaluators Score Proposals

Evaluation

Evaluation Factors

  • Technical Approach (often 40-50%)
  • Past Performance (often 20-30%)
  • Price (varies widely)
  • Management Approach
  • Key Personnel

Rating Scale (Common)

  • Outstanding / Exceptional
  • Good / Acceptable
  • Marginal / Acceptable
  • Unacceptable

Key Insight

Section L tells you what to submit. Section M tells you how it will be scored. Read both carefully.

GovCon Giants Session 1: Fundamentals

Three Levels of Proposal Quality

Framework

1. Compliant

Follows all format requirements, answers all questions, meets page limits. The minimum bar.

2. Responsive

Directly addresses evaluation criteria. Shows you understood what they asked for.

3. Competitive

Demonstrates clear discriminators. Shows why you're the BEST choice, not just A choice.

Your Goal

Every proposal must be compliant. Most should be responsive. Winners are always competitive.

GovCon Giants Session 2: Simplified Acquisitions

Quote vs. Proposal

Definitions

Quote (RFQ)

  • Simplified acquisitions
  • Commercial items (FAR Part 12)
  • Usually price-focused
  • Shorter format
  • Uses SF 1449 often
  • Faster turnaround

Proposal (RFP)

  • Negotiated procurements
  • Multiple evaluation factors
  • Technical volumes required
  • More complex format
  • Uses SF 33 typically
  • Longer evaluation period
GovCon Giants Session 2: Simplified Acquisitions

Simplified Acquisition Response Format

Template
  • Cover Letter: Company info, POC, SAM status
  • Technical Capability: How you meet requirements
  • Relevant Experience: 2-3 similar projects
  • Pricing: Line items with rates/totals
  • Certifications & Reps: Required clauses signed

Keep It Tight

Simplified doesn't mean sloppy. It means concise, focused, and easy for the evaluator to score.

GovCon Giants Session 2: Simplified Acquisitions

LPTA vs. Best Value

Strategy

LPTA (Lowest Price Technically Acceptable)

  • Price is the deciding factor
  • Technical is pass/fail
  • Meet requirements - nothing more
  • Don't over-engineer
  • Focus on lowest compliant price

Best Value

  • Technical quality matters
  • Higher price can win
  • Discriminators count
  • Emphasize strengths
  • Worth investing in quality

Know Your Evaluation Type

Your entire strategy changes based on whether it's LPTA or Best Value. Check Section M carefully.

Technical Approach &
Past Performance

11:15 AM - 12:30 PM (75 min)

Your technical approach tells the
evaluator HOW you'll do the work

Not what you CAN do. Not what you've DONE. How you WILL deliver on THIS contract.

Technical Approach Structure

Framework
  • Understanding: Demonstrate you grasp the requirement
  • Approach: Your methodology to accomplish objectives
  • Management: How you'll manage the contract
  • Staffing: Key personnel and organization
  • Quality Control: How you ensure deliverables meet standards
GovCon Giants Session 3: Technical Approach

Writing the Understanding Section

Technical

Do This

  • Restate key objectives in your words
  • Identify critical success factors
  • Note potential challenges
  • Show you've done your research

Don't Do This

  • Copy-paste from the RFP
  • Use generic statements
  • Ignore agency context
  • Miss key requirements

The Goal

"This offeror clearly understands what we need and why it matters."

GovCon Giants Session 3: Technical Approach

Developing Win Themes

Strategy
  • Win themes are compelling reasons to choose YOU
  • Each theme should connect to evaluation criteria
  • Repeat themes throughout your proposal
  • Support themes with evidence (data, experience)
  • 3-5 themes maximum per proposal

Example Win Theme

"Our proven rapid deployment methodology reduces implementation time by 40%, ensuring the agency achieves mission objectives faster."

GovCon Giants Session 3: Technical Approach

Past Performance: The Proof

Evidence
  • Past performance proves you can deliver what you promise
  • Relevance matters more than recency
  • Demonstrate similar scope, size, and complexity
  • Include problems solved and results achieved
  • Government contracts carry more weight than commercial

No Federal Experience?

Use state/local government, subcontracts, or commercial contracts with similar scope. Frame relevance clearly.

GovCon Giants Session 3: Technical Approach

The STAR Method for Past Performance

Format

S - Situation

Describe the client's challenge or need you were hired to address.

T - Task

Explain your specific responsibilities and the scope of work.

A - Action

Detail the approach and methods you used to accomplish the task.

R - Result

Quantify the outcomes: savings, improvements, metrics achieved.

Relevance Statement

Explicitly connect this experience to the current requirement: "This experience demonstrates our ability to..."

2:30 PM - 3:30 PM (60 min)

Task orders are where the
real money flows

Having a contract vehicle (GSA Schedule, GWAC) is just the start. Winning task orders is the goal.

Task Order vs. Open Market Proposals

Comparison

Task Order Proposals

  • Compete against schedule holders
  • Faster turnaround (often 7-14 days)
  • Already pre-vetted via contract
  • Pricing already negotiated
  • Shorter technical responses
  • eBuy is primary platform

Open Market (SAM.gov)

  • Compete against everyone
  • Longer timelines (30-60+ days)
  • Must prove qualifications
  • Negotiate pricing each time
  • Full proposal volumes
  • Posted on SAM.gov
GovCon Giants Session 5: Task Orders

eBuy Response Format

Template
  • Cover Letter with GSA Schedule info
  • Technical Approach (usually 5-10 pages)
  • Relevant Experience (2-3 examples)
  • Key Personnel resumes
  • Pricing based on your schedule rates

Turnaround Time

eBuy responses typically have 7-14 day deadlines. Have your templates ready BEFORE opportunities post.

GovCon Giants Session 5: Task Orders

Task Order Pricing Strategy

Pricing
  • Your GSA rates are the CEILING, not the floor
  • Discounting is expected for competitive task orders
  • Volume discounts can win larger awards
  • Consider blended rates for teams
  • Price to win, but protect your margins

The Balance

Task orders often go to lowest price technically acceptable (LPTA). Know when to compete on price vs. quality.
