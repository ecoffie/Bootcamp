Position Yourself as the Solution

Action
  • Lead with how you solve THEIR problem, not what you sell
  • Speak to their pain in your capability statement

Sources Sought & RFI Explained

Basics

Sources Sought

  • Agency is researching the market
  • "Who can do this work?"
  • Determines if set-aside is possible
  • LOW effort, HIGH impact

RFI (Request for Info)

  • Agency needs input on approach
  • "How should we structure this?"
  • Your response shapes the RFP
  • Builds relationship with buyer

Why This Matters

Responding to Sources Sought can influence how agencies structure contracts - potentially in YOUR favor.

Expiring Contracts:
Find Your Next Win

3:30 PM - 4:30 PM (60 min)

The fastest path to contracts:
Target recompetes

Someone already proved the agency buys this. Position before the competition knows about it.

Two Paths: Team or Compete

Strategy

* Team with Incumbent

  • Reach out as potential subcontractor
  • Offer your certification or capability
  • Help them meet small biz requirements
  • Lower risk entry point
  • Build past performance

* Compete Against Them

  • Research incumbent performance
  • Identify gaps or weaknesses
  • Position as the better option
  • Contact the Contracting Officer
  • Prepare for the recompete RFP

Common Question

"Do I reach out to the recipient who won the award or the POC on SAM?" It depends on your strategy - we'll cover both approaches.

GovCon Giants Session 7: Expiring Contracts

Using the Expiring Contracts Sheet

Tool
  • Filter by your NAICS codes to find relevant contracts
  • Sort by expiration date (6-18 months out is ideal)
  • Identify incumbent contractor and contract value
  • Find the Contracting Officer contact info
  • Research the incumbent's performance history

Timing Is Everything

Start positioning 12-18 months before expiration. 6 months is often too late.
