Path for staffing: GSA ‚ÄúSchedule‚Äù

  • GSA is a federal ‚Äúbuyer support‚Äù agency that runs popular contract catalogs agencies can purchase from
  • MAS (Multiple Award Schedule) is one of those catalogs (often called ‚Äúthe GSA Schedule‚Äù)
  • SIN is a category code inside MAS (like an aisle in a store)
  • For temporary staffing, the key category is MAS SIN 561320 (Temporary Staffing)
  • Some buys may be set aside for small business under 561320SBSA (small business set-aside)

Why this matters: Agencies already use this lane ‚Äî it can be faster than ‚Äúopen market‚Äù buys.

Source: GSA MAS Temporary Staffing Services slipsheet (SIN 561320SBSA), July 2020.

How a staffing buy works on GSA MAS (simple)

  • Agency need: ‚ÄúWe need 10 admin staff in [location] for 90‚Äì120 days.‚Äù
  • RFQ: agency posts a request for quotes (often in eBuy) under SIN 561320
  • Quotes: eligible Schedule contractors submit rates and staffing plans
  • Award: agency issues an order (sometimes sets up a BPA for repeated staffing needs)
  • Performance: you recruit, onboard, fill shifts, and invoice per contract terms

Key idea: You win by being fast, compliant, and reliable ‚Äî and by having a clear staffing process.

How to get on GSA MAS and access eBuy

  • Step 1 ‚Äî Register: complete your SAM.gov registration (UEI) and keep it active
  • Step 2 ‚Äî Training: complete GSA‚Äôs mandatory Pathways to Success training (required for new MAS offerors)
  • Step 3 ‚Äî Submit an offer: use eOffer (with a FAS ID) to submit your MAS offer under the right SIN(s)
  • Step 4 ‚Äî After award: ensure you‚Äôre an Authorized Negotiator, then register your contract on the Vendor Support Center (VSC)
  • Step 5 ‚Äî eBuy access: to participate in eBuy, you must (1) register on VSC and (2) post your MAS catalog on GSA Advantage!

Expectation: Getting a Schedule is a process (not a weekend task). Start now while you build pipeline through SAM.gov and teaming.

Sources: GSA ‚ÄúRoadmap to get a MAS contract‚Äù (updated Nov 25, 2025) and GSA VSC guidance on eBuy access.

Compliance basics: Service Contract Labor Standards (SCLS/SCA)

  • SCA is the Service Contract Act (the law behind many wage/fringe requirements). In the FAR you‚Äôll often see SCLS (Service Contract Labor Standards).
  • Many staffing/service contracts include FAR 52.222-41 (Service Contract Labor Standards)
  • You must pay required minimum wages + fringe benefits per the contract‚Äôs wage determination
  • If a labor category isn‚Äôt listed, you may need an added classification request (commonly via SF 1444)
  • Flow-down applies to subcontractors too

Bottom line: A staffing GovCon win is often won on recruiting + compliance + reliability, not fancy marketing.

A capability statement is a 1‚Äì2 page ‚Äúbusiness resume‚Äù used in government contracting to introduce your company quickly.

  • When you use it: outreach emails, networking events, Sources Sought/RFI responses, and early pipeline conversations
