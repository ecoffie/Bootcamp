The Goal of Your Cap Statement

Get a MEETING

Not a contract. Not an award. Just a conversation.
That's it. Everything else comes after.

The 6 Essential Sections

1. Company Header

Logo, name, tagline, contact info, CAGE/UEI

2. Core Competencies

3-5 specific things you do BEST

3. Differentiators

What makes you DIFFERENT from competitors

4. Past Performance

2-3 relevant contracts with results

5. Company Data

NAICS, certifications, clearances, size

6. Call to Action

What do you want them to DO next?

1

Company Header

First impression. Make it count.

✓
Professional logo (high resolution)
✓
Company name prominently displayed
✓
Tagline that explains what you DO
✓
Contact: Name, phone, email, website
✓
CAGE Code and UEI number
2

Core Competencies

What you do BEST. Be specific.

DON'T

"IT Services"

"Professional Services"

"Quality Solutions"

DO

"Cloud Migration for Legacy DoD Systems"

"CMMC Level 2 Compliance Consulting"

"24/7 Cybersecurity Monitoring & Response"

3

Differentiators

Why should they choose YOU over 1,000 other companies?

✓
Unique methodology or approach
✓
Specialized certifications or clearances
✓
Geographic advantage or local presence
✓
Speed, flexibility, or capacity
✓
Specific mission experience
4

Past Performance

Proof that you can deliver. Results matter.

CLIENT + PROBLEM + SOLUTION = RESULT
"Helped [CLIENT] solve [PROBLEM] by [SOLUTION], resulting in [MEASURABLE RESULT]"
Example

"Migrated Department of Army's legacy HR system to AWS cloud, reducing downtime by 94% and saving $2.3M annually in maintenance costs."

5

Company Data

The info they NEED to verify and contact you.

Required

  • CAGE Code
  • UEI Number
  • Primary NAICS codes (top 3-5)
  • Business size (small, 8(a), etc.)
  • Certifications (WOSB, SDVOSB, HUBZone)

If Applicable

  • Security clearances held
  • GSA Schedule number
  • Contract vehicles
  • Bonding capacity
  • Insurance limits
6

Call to Action

Tell them EXACTLY what to do next.

WEAK

"Contact us for more information"

"We look forward to working with you"

STRONG

"Schedule a 15-minute call to discuss your cloud migration challenges: calendly.com/yourcompany"

"Email john@company.com with your current IT modernization priorities"

The #1 Secret to Winning

Agency-Specific
Customization

One generic cap statement = zero responses.
Tailored cap statements = meetings.

How To Customize For Each Agency

1
Research the agency's strategic plan and priorities
2
Identify their specific pain points (Week 2!)
3
Use THEIR terminology and acronyms
4
Reference THEIR mission in your core competencies
5
Show relevant past performance (similar agencies)

Before & After: Core Competencies

GENERIC

"IT Services and Solutions"

"Cybersecurity Services"

"Cloud Computing"

FOR DHS

"Border Security Technology Integration"

"EINSTEIN 3A Cybersecurity Implementation"

"FedRAMP High Cloud Migration for Sensitive Systems"

Same company. Same capabilities. DIFFERENT positioning.

Design Matters: The 7-Second Test

✓
ONE page only - no exceptions
✓
Clear visual hierarchy - most important info first
✓
Plenty of white space - don't crowd it
✓
Consistent colors and fonts - look professional
✓
Scannable - bullets, not paragraphs

5 Mistakes That Kill Your Chances

1
Multiple pages - they won't read past page 1
2
No CAGE/UEI - looks unprofessional
3
Outdated information - check dates on past performance
4
Buzzwords without proof - "world-class" means nothing
5
Poor quality PDF - fuzzy logos, broken formatting
