What Is An Appropriations Bill?

DEFINITION
A law that gives federal agencies permission to SPEND money
HOW MANY
12 bills fund the entire federal government each fiscal year (Oct 1 - Sep 30)
THE 12 BILLS
Defense | Homeland Security | HHS/Labor | Agriculture | Commerce/Justice
Energy/Water | Interior | MilCon/VA | State/Foreign | Transportation/HUD
Financial Services | Legislative Branch
WHY IT MATTERS
No appropriations bill = no new contracts, no new programs, no spending
FOR YOU
Track which bills pass - that tells you which agencies can spend!
1B

CURRENT
9 of 12 appropriations bills still unfunded - CR keeps gov't at FY2025 levels
THE PROBLEM

OPPORTUNITY
DoD, HHS, Labor still waiting for full-year funding - MASSIVE pent-up demand
WHAT TO WATCH

ACTION
Position NOW - when full funding passes, contracts will release FAST

How To Position Yourself

Make Yourself Easy to FIND

  • Complete SAM.gov registration
  • Register in agency vendor systems
  • Attend industry days & matchmaking
  • Contact OSDBU proactively
  • Submit capability statements BEFORE opportunities

Make Capabilities Easy to MATCH

  • Research agency missions first
  • Create agency-SPECIFIC cap statements
  • Use THEIR terminology
  • Reference THEIR strategic plans
  • Present as solutions to THEIR pain points

What NOT To Do

DON'T SAY

"We provide IT services"

"What contracts do you have?"

"We can do anything"

DO SAY

"We help agencies modernize legacy systems quickly, reducing security risks"

"What are your biggest challenges in [area]? How can we help?"

"We specialize in [specific capability] for [specific mission]"

Your 4-Week Action Plan (Weeks 1-2)

Week 1

  • Complete business profile assessment
  • Identify your top 3 strengths
  • List all certifications
  • Assess current capacity

Week 2

  • Research top 5 matching agencies
  • Identify their pain points
  • Match pain points to capabilities
  • Score each agency

Your 4-Week Action Plan (Weeks 3-4)

Week 3

  • Create agency priority list
  • Research OSDBU contacts
  • Prepare agency-specific cap statements
  • Find opportunities in SAM.gov

Week 4

  • Contact OSDBU from top 5
  • Reference pain points in emails
  • Offer solutions, not services
  • Follow up with opportunities
