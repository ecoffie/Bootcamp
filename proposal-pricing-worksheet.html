Proposal Pricing Worksheet

Build compliant, competitive pricing for federal proposals

Step 1: Identify Contract Type

The contract type determines how you structure your pricing. Check the RFP Section L (Instructions) for the required format.

Firm Fixed Price (FFP)

You quote a total price. You absorb cost overruns but keep savings. Most common for well-defined requirements.

Best for: Clear scope, low risk
Time & Materials (T&M)

You quote hourly rates + materials markup. Government pays actual hours worked. Used when scope is uncertain.

Best for: Uncertain scope, variable work
Cost-Plus (CPFF/CPAF)

Government reimburses all allowable costs plus a fee. Requires certified accounting system. Used for R&D and complex work.

Best for: Complex, high-risk work

LPTA vs. Best Value

  • LPTA (Lowest Price Technically Acceptable): Price is king. Meet minimum requirements at lowest cost.
  • Best Value: Balance of price and technical. You can win with higher price if technical approach is superior.

Pricing Compliance Checklist

Before submitting, verify your pricing meets all requirements.

  • Format matches RFP instructions - Pricing spreadsheet/format matches Section L exactly
  • All CLINs priced - Every Contract Line Item Number has a price (no blanks)
  • Math is correct - All calculations verified, totals match
  • All years included - Base year AND all option years priced
  • Labor categories match PWS - Categories align with Performance Work Statement requirements
  • Rates are supportable - You can justify every rate if asked
  • No unallowable costs - No entertainment, alcohol, lobbying, or other FAR-prohibited costs
  • Travel follows regulations - Per diem rates match GSA, lodging within limits
  • Subcontractor pricing included - If teaming, sub costs are incorporated
  • Price is realistic - Not so low it raises red flags about ability to perform
  • Certifications complete - All required pricing certifications signed
  • Electronic file works - Excel formulas function, PDF is readable

Common Pricing Mistakes to Avoid

Mistakes That Lose Contracts

  • Pricing too low: Government may reject as "unrealistic" or question your ability to perform
  • Missing line items: Leaving any CLIN blank = non-responsive = rejection
  • Wrong format: Not using the exact format requested in Section L
  • Math errors: Rates x Hours doesn't equal total = automatic concern
  • Forgetting escalation: Option years at same price as base year looks suspicious
  • Underestimating hours: Technical approach requires 5 people but you priced 2
  • Missing ODCs: Technical says "travel required" but no travel in pricing
  • Ignoring the PWS: Pricing doesn't reflect the actual work required

Quick Reference: FAR Profit Guidelines

While profit isn't capped, these are typical ranges the government considers reasonable:

Contract Type Typical Profit Range Notes
Firm Fixed Price 10% - 15% Higher risk = higher acceptable profit
Time & Materials 8% - 12% Lower risk = lower profit expectation
Cost Plus Fixed Fee 6% - 10% Fee is negotiated, not based on cost
Cost Plus Award Fee 3% base + up to 10% award Award fee based on performance
