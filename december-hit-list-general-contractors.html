üí° Tips for General Contractors:

1. Site visits are critical - Most construction projects require mandatory site visits. Plan ahead!

2. Bonding requirements - Many construction contracts require bid bonds, performance bonds, and payment bonds. Have your bonding capacity ready.

3. Check NAICS codes - Make sure your business is registered with the correct NAICS codes in SAM.gov

4. Licensing matters - Verify state/local licensing requirements for the project location

5. Set-asides advantage - If you're SDVOSB, 8(a), HUBZone, or WOSB certified, these opportunities have less competition

6. Read the full solicitation - Construction specs are detailed. Download all drawings, specs, and attachments from SAM.gov

7. Ask questions early - Use the RFI (Request for Information) process. Submit questions well before the deadline

8. Total opportunities available: This list shows the top 25 construction opportunities. Check SAM.gov for all 237 construction-related opportunities!
