Win Theme Development Worksheet

Create compelling discriminators that set you apart from competitors

What Are Win Themes?

Definition

Win themes are the compelling reasons the government should select YOUR company over all other bidders. They are the key messages that run throughout your proposal, supported by evidence.

Characteristics of Strong Win Themes

  • Directly tied to evaluation criteria in Section M
  • Specific to THIS opportunity, not generic capabilities
  • Supported by evidence (past performance, data, certifications)
  • Focused on benefits to the government, not just features
  • Differentiate you from likely competitors
  • Repeated throughout the proposal in different sections

Common Mistake

Win themes are NOT "We have great customer service" or "We deliver quality work." Every bidder says that. Win themes must be SPECIFIC and PROVABLE.

Win Theme Examples

Example: IT Services Contract

Weak Theme: "We provide excellent IT support."

Generic. Every bidder claims this. No proof.

Strong Theme: "Our 98.7% first-call resolution rate, achieved across 15 federal help desk contracts, reduces escalation costs and gets users back to work faster."

Specific, measurable, tied to cost savings, supported by evidence.

Example: Facilities Maintenance

Weak Theme: "We have experienced maintenance technicians."

Generic. No differentiation.

Strong Theme: "Our predictive maintenance program, which reduced emergency repairs by 67% at VA Medical Centers, minimizes facility downtime and extends equipment life."

Specific methodology, quantified results, relevant agency experience.

Example: Consulting Services

Weak Theme: "We deliver projects on time and on budget."

Expected. Not a differentiator.

Strong Theme: "Our three key personnel have collectively supported this program for 12 years, eliminating ramp-up time and ensuring continuity of institutional knowledge on Day 1."

Specific to this opportunity, addresses transition risk, focuses on key personnel strength.

Integration Tip

Each major theme should appear at least 3-4 times throughout your proposal. Use different wording each time, but the core message should be consistent. Themes create a "red thread" that evaluators remember.

Win Theme Quality Checklist

Before finalizing your themes, verify each one passes these tests:

  • Is it specific to THIS opportunity, not generic?
  • Is it tied to evaluation criteria in Section M?
  • Do you have concrete evidence to support it?
  • Does it focus on benefits to the government?
  • Would a competitor struggle to make the same claim?
  • Can you weave it throughout multiple proposal sections?
  • Is it memorable and easy to understand?
