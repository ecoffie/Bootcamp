What Is A Continuing Resolution?

Continuing Resolution (CR)
A temporary funding bill that keeps the government running at LAST YEAR'S spending levels when Congress can't agree on a new budget.

Think of it as the government hitting the "PAUSE" button on new spending.

How Budgets Are SUPPOSED to Work

FEB

APR-SEP

Congress Debates & Passes Bills

12 appropriations bills fund different parts of government

Agencies have new budgets and can spend on new priorities

What Happens During a CR?

1
Agencies operate at LAST YEAR'S funding levels
2
NO new programs or initiatives can start
3
NO new multi-year contracts can be awarded
4
Hiring freezes at many agencies
5
Existing contracts continue—but cautiously

How CRs Affect Government Contractors

The BAD News

  • New contract awards delayed
  • Proposal evaluations slow down
  • Agencies hesitant to spend
  • Uncertainty kills planning
  • Small businesses hit hardest

The GOOD News

  • Existing contracts continue
  • Task orders still flow
  • IDIQ vehicles keep moving
  • Agencies still NEED services
  • End-of-CR spending surges

CR vs. Shutdown: Know The Difference

Continuing Resolution

  • Government stays OPEN
  • Employees get paid
  • Contractors get paid
  • Just limited to old budget
  • Operations continue normally

Government Shutdown

  • Non-essential workers furloughed
  • Contractors STOP getting paid
  • Contract work may halt
  • Invoices pile up unpaid
  • Massive disruption
