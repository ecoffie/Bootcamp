February 2026 Hit List

Proposal-Ready Opportunities for Practice & Wins

Proposal Writing Surge Companion

How to Use This Hit List

These opportunities are curated specifically for proposal writing practice and beginner-to-intermediate contractors:

  • Simplified Acquisitions - Great for first-time proposers (shorter responses)
  • Sources Sought - Low-risk way to get on agency radar before RFPs drop
  • Full & Open (Small) - Good practice for competitive proposal writing
  • Task Orders - For those with GSA Schedules or GWAC access

Tip: Use these opportunities during the Proposal Writing Surge workshops to practice building compliance matrices and drafting technical approaches.

Simplified Acquisitions (Under $250K)

Perfect for beginners - shorter proposals, faster decisions

#1
IT Support Services - Help Desk Operations
Simplified Small Business Beginner Friendly
Agency Department of Agriculture
Est. Value $75,000 - $150,000
NAICS 541512 - Computer Systems Design
Response Due March 15, 2026

Provide Tier 1 and Tier 2 help desk support for USDA regional office. Services include phone support, ticket management, basic troubleshooting, and escalation procedures. 12-month base with two 12-month options.

Why This is Good for Practice

Clear, straightforward requirements. Common IT service scope that many small businesses can perform. Good for building compliance matrix skills with manageable complexity.

Sources Sought & RFI Notices

Low-risk responses to get on agency radar before RFPs drop

#5
Cybersecurity Assessment Services - Sources Sought
Sources Sought Small Business
Agency Department of Homeland Security
Est. Value $500,000 - $2,000,000
NAICS 541512 - Computer Systems Design
Response Due March 5, 2026

DHS is seeking information from small businesses capable of providing cybersecurity vulnerability assessments, penetration testing, and security compliance audits. Agency is determining if this requirement can be set aside for small business.

Why This is Good for Practice

Sources Sought responses are shorter (typically 5-10 pages) and lower stakes. Your response could influence how the agency structures the eventual RFP. Great way to practice capability statements.

#6
Facilities Maintenance - RFI
RFI HUBZone
Agency Department of Veterans Affairs
Est. Value $1,000,000 - $3,000,000
NAICS 561210 - Facilities Support Services
Response Due March 8, 2026

VA Medical Center seeking information on facilities maintenance capabilities including HVAC, electrical, plumbing, and general building maintenance. Interested in understanding small business capacity for 5-year IDIQ structure.

Why This is Good for Practice

RFI responses let you provide input on contract structure. HUBZone set-aside reduces competition. Responding now positions you for the eventual solicitation.

#7
Professional Staffing Services - Market Research
Sources Sought WOSB
Agency Department of Health and Human Services
Est. Value $2,000,000 - $5,000,000
NAICS 561320 - Temporary Help Services
Response Due March 12, 2026

HHS seeking information from WOSB-certified companies capable of providing professional staffing services including project managers, business analysts, and administrative support for multiple program offices.

Perfect for staffing companies. WOSB focus means less competition. Sources Sought response is great practice for the capability summary section of larger proposals.

For contractors with existing contract vehicles
