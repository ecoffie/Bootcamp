Strategic Report: How Agencies Struggle to Find and Match Small Business Capabilities
Government Contracting Bootcamp
Date: January 2026

Executive Summary

Federal agencies consistently report two primary pain points when working with small businesses:

1. "We have a hard time finding small businesses"

2. "Matching capabilities is challenging"

These pain points create significant opportunities for small businesses that understand how to position themselves effectively. This report details what agencies say about these challenges and provides actionable solutions for small businesses to address them.

üéØ The Core Challenge

Key Finding: Agencies WANT to work with small businesses to meet their 23% contracting goals, but they struggle with finding qualified small businesses and matching capabilities to mission requirements. Small businesses that address these pain points gain significant competitive advantages.

üìã Pain Point #1: Finding Small Businesses

What Agencies Say

"We struggle to locate qualified small businesses with the right capabilities"
"Small businesses aren't registered where we can find them"
"We don't know where to look for qualified small business vendors"
"We have limited visibility into small business capabilities"
"It's difficult to discover small businesses that can meet our requirements"

Why This Happens

  • Small businesses aren't always registered in SAM.gov
  • Agencies rely on databases that may be incomplete
  • Small businesses don't proactively reach out to agencies
  • SBLOs don't have comprehensive vendor directories
  • Small businesses aren't visible in industry days or matchmaking events
  • Incomplete vendor profiles in agency procurement systems

Your Opportunity: Make Yourself Easy to Find

Registration & Visibility

  • Complete SAM.gov registration - Keep it current and comprehensive
  • Register in agency vendor systems - Don't wait for solicitations
  • Participate in vendor directories - Get listed where agencies look
  • Attend industry days - Increase visibility and face-to-face contact
  • Join matchmaking events - Connect directly with SBLOs
  • Participate in small business outreach - Be where agencies look

Proactive Outreach

  • Contact SBLOs directly - Don't wait for them to find you
  • Submit capability statements proactively - Before opportunities are announced
  • Follow up regularly - Stay on their radar
  • Request meetings - Build relationships before opportunities arise

üîó Pain Point #2: Matching Capabilities

What Agencies Say

"It's challenging to match small business capabilities to our mission requirements"
"We can't easily identify which small businesses have the technical expertise we need"
"Capability statements are often too generic or don't clearly show how they help our mission"
"We struggle to connect small business offerings to our specific needs"
"Small businesses don't clearly communicate how they solve our problems"

Why This Happens

  • Capability statements are too generic ("we do everything")
  • Small businesses don't research agency missions before reaching out
  • Capabilities aren't presented in terms of agency pain points
  • Technical expertise isn't clearly matched to agency needs
  • Small businesses don't use agency-specific language or terminology
  • Lack of clear connection between offerings and mission requirements

Your Opportunity: Make Capabilities Easy to Match

Research First

  • Study agency missions - Understand what they do
  • Review strategic plans - Know their priorities
  • Research recent contracts - See what they buy
  • Attend industry days - Learn their language and needs
  • Read budget requests - Understand their funding priorities

Tailor Your Approach

  • Create agency-specific capability statements - Not generic ones
  • Use agency terminology - Speak their language
  • Reference strategic plans - Show you understand their mission
  • Present capabilities in terms of pain points - Solve their problems
  • Show specific examples - Connect capabilities to mission needs
  • Match technical expertise to requirements - Be specific, not general

üéØ Agency Matching Framework

Step 1: Research Agency Mission

Questions to Answer:

  • What is the agency's primary mission?
  • What are their current strategic priorities?
  • What are their biggest challenges?
  • What types of contracts do they award?
  • What NAICS codes do they use most?

Step 2: Identify Pain Points

Questions to Answer:

  • What keeps agency leadership awake at night?
  • What are their biggest operational challenges?
  • What capabilities do they struggle to find?
  • Where are their capability gaps?
  • What do their recent contracts tell you about needs?

Step 3: Match Your Capabilities

Questions to Answer:

  • How does your expertise address their mission?
  • Which of their pain points do you solve?
  • How does your experience relate to their needs?
  • What specific value do you provide?
  • Why should they work with you?

Step 4: Tailor Your Communication

Questions to Answer:

  • How should you present your capabilities?
  • What language should you use?
  • What examples are most relevant?
  • How do you connect to their strategic plans?
  • What makes you easy to match to their needs?

üìä Common Matching Challenges & Solutions

Challenge Problem Agency Perspective Solution
Generic Capability Statements Small businesses send generic "we do everything" statements "This doesn't tell me how you help our mission" Research agency mission, create agency-specific statements, present capabilities as solutions, use agency terminology
Missing Information Small businesses don't provide enough detail "I can't tell if this matches our needs" Include specific technical qualifications, relevant past performance, list relevant NAICS codes, show clear connection to mission
Wrong Timing Small businesses reach out only when opportunities are announced "It's too late - we've already identified potential vendors" Reach out proactively, submit capability statements regularly, build relationships with SBLOs in advance, stay on their radar
No Visibility Small businesses aren't where agencies look for vendors "We don't know you exist" Complete SAM.gov registration, register in agency vendor systems, attend industry days, participate in small business outreach

üöÄ Action Plan: Making Yourself Easy to Find & Match

Week 1: Registration & Visibility

  • Complete/update SAM.gov registration (comprehensive profile)
  • Register in target agency vendor systems
  • Get listed in relevant vendor directories
  • Sign up for agency small business newsletters

Week 2: Research & Preparation

  • Research target agency missions and strategic plans
  • Identify agency pain points and priorities
  • Review recent contracts and opportunities
  • Understand agency terminology and language

Week 3: Capability Statement Development

  • Create agency-specific capability statements (one per target agency)
  • Match capabilities to agency pain points
  • Use agency terminology and reference strategic plans
  • Include specific examples relevant to their mission

Week 4: Proactive Outreach

  • Contact SBLOs from target agencies
  • Submit capability statements proactively
  • Request meetings to discuss capabilities
  • Register for upcoming industry days

Ongoing: Relationship Building

  • Follow up regularly with SBLOs
  • Update capability statements as agency needs evolve
  • Participate in industry days and matchmaking events
  • Stay on agency radar with periodic updates

üí¨ Sample Outreach Messages

For Agencies Struggling to Find Small Businesses

Subject: Small Business Capabilities - [Your Company] - [Agency Name]


Message:

"I understand [Agency Name] is actively seeking small businesses with [specific capability]. We are a [certification] certified small business specializing in [specific expertise relevant to their mission].

We've reviewed [Agency Name]'s strategic plan and believe our capabilities directly support your [specific mission area/goal]. Specifically, we help agencies [solve specific pain point you identified].

I've attached our capability statement tailored to [Agency Name]'s mission. I'd welcome the opportunity to discuss how we can support your small business goals and mission requirements.

Would you be available for a brief call to discuss our capabilities?"

For Capability Matching

Subject: Capability Match - [Specific Mission Area] - [Your Company]


Message:

"I've researched [Agency Name]'s mission and noticed your focus on [specific area]. Our expertise in [specific capability] directly addresses [specific challenge/pain point you identified].

We understand that matching capabilities to mission requirements can be challenging. To make this easier, I've prepared a capability statement that:

  • References your strategic plan priorities
  • Shows how our [specific expertise] supports your [specific mission area]
  • Includes examples of similar work for [relevant agencies/programs]

I'd welcome the opportunity to discuss how our capabilities align with your mission requirements and help you meet your small business goals."
